# Z-Plane Eclipsing When Scaling Cards With Camera Journal v0.1
**Date:** 2026-03-30
**Session:** Discovered during cross-browser resize sync design
**Status:** Draft
**Preceding:** none
**Changes from v0.0:** Initial journal — captured from design.04 §3.4

## Problem

In a camera-only model, making a card "larger" means dollying it closer to the camera (smaller Z distance). But a large card far away (high Z) can appear large in the viewport while being geometrically behind small cards that are close (low Z). The large-far card renders behind the small-close card — eclipsed.

## Why This Matters

Users expect "make this bigger" to make the card visually prominent. In 3D, "bigger" and "in front" are coupled via Z distance. A card that's visually large but geometrically far can be hidden behind geometrically close but visually small cards.

## Approaches Considered

### A) Accept the constraint
True 3D behavior. Closer objects occlude farther ones. Users learn that "larger" means "closer" and occluding is expected. The card IS in front.

### B) Scale card DOM instead of dollying
When the user wants "bigger," resize the card DOM (change CSS3DObject scale or calcCardSize parameters). Card stays at same Z, so depth ordering preserved. Breaks camera-only model.

### C) Hybrid: per-group z-index
Within a focus group, all cards share a narrow Z band. Active card gets z-index boost within the group. On unfocus/return-to-ring, z-index cleared. Managed per group, not globally.

## The z-index Problem Cascade

1. Set z-index on large-far card → renders in front of eclipsing card
2. But now renders in front of ALL closer cards
3. Is it permanent? When clear?
4. What triggers clear? Unfocus? Deselect? Ring return?
5. Do we poll all on-screen cards to compute ordering?
6. Per-group sorting needed?

## Current Decision

Deferred. Not a blocker for cross-browser resize sync. The Z-slide removal (Task #4) reduces Z manipulation in focus groups. With frustum layout keeping focused cards at similar Z depths, eclipsing is minimal in practice.

Revisit if it becomes a UX problem. Overlaps with Task #5 (intuitive card sizing) and Task #10 (this task).
